In a sense, criticism is one of the most important tasks a manager has. Yet it's also one of the most dreaded and put off. And, too many managers have poorly mastered the crucial art of feedback. this deficiency has a great cost: How criticisms are given and received goes a long way in determining how satisfied people are with their work, with those they work with, and with those to whom they are responsible.

Many managers are too wiling to criticize, but frugal with praise, leaving their employees feeling that they only hear about how they're doing when they make a mistake. This propensity to criticism is compounded by managers who delay giving any feedback at all for long periods.
